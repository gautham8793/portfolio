About Me

Curious first.
Designer second.

I studied Textile Technology at Anna University — not design, not computer science. But I was always drawn to how things work for people, not just how they work. That restlessness led me to UX.

Over the last 7+ years, I've designed for genuinely hard problems — making ML-generated retail forecasts legible to merchants, unifying IoT experiences across white-label clients, redesigning hospital workflows to reduce cognitive overload.

What changed recently is that I stopped just designing for AI and started building with it. Using Claude, Cursor and other AI tools, I've prototyped and shipped things I never could have done alone. That shift — from designer to designer-who-ships — is what drives me now.

I'm looking for a role where that combination is valued: strategic design thinking, deep UX craft, and the ability to move fast and build.
